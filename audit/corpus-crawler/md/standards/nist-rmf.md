### Overview
Recent Updates

**August 27, 2025:** In response to [Executive Order 14306](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/06/11/2025-10804/sustaining-select-efforts-to-strengthen-the-nations-cybersecurity-and-amending-executive-order-13694), [NIST SP 800-53 Release 5.2.0](/projects/cprt/catalog#/cprt/framework/version/SP_800_53_5_2_0/home) has been finalized and is now available on the Cybersecurity and Privacy Reference Tool. Release 5.2.0 includes changes to SP 800-53 and [SP 800-53A](/projects/cprt/catalog#/cprt/framework/version/SP_800_53_A_5_2_0/home), there are no changes to the baselines in [SP 800-53B](/projects/cprt/catalog#/cprt/framework/version/SP_800_53_B_5_2_0/home). A [summary of the changes](/csrc/media/Projects/risk-management/800-53%20Comment%20Site/SP800-53-r5.2.0-changes.pdf) is available, and replaces the "preview version" issued on August 22 (no longer available).

**August 22, 2025:**A preview of the updates to NIST SP 800-53 (Release 5.2.0) is available on the [Public Comment Site](/Projects/risk-management/sp800-53-controls/public-comments#/search/awaiting). This preview will be available until NIST issues Release 5.2.0 through the [Cybersecurity and Privacy Reference Tool](/projects/cprt/catalog#/cprt/home). SP 800-53 Release 5.2.0 will include:

- New Control/Control Enhancements and Assessment Procedures: SA-15(13), SA-24, SI-02(07)
- Revisions to Existing Controls: SI-07(12)
- Updates to Control Discussion: SA-04, SA-05, SA-08, SA-08(14), SI-02, SI-02(05)
- Updates to Related Controls: All -01 Controls, AU-02, AU-03, CA-07, IR-04, IR-06, IR-08, SA-15, SI-02, SI-07

**August 14, 2025:**The [NIST SP 800-53 Control Overlays for Securing AI Systems Concept Paper](https://csrc.nist.gov/csrc/media/Projects/cosais/documents/NIST-Overlays-SecuringAI-concept-paper.pdf) is available for comment, and we welcome stakeholders to join the [NIST Overlays Securing AI Systems Slack Collaboration](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cosais/slack) to engage in facilitated discussions with the NIST principal investigators and other subgroup members, share ideas, provide real-time feedback, and contribute to overlay development.

**August 6, 2025:** The expedited public comment period on the NIST SP 800-53 controls is closed. Thank you for your feedback! We expect to issue SP 800-53 Release 5.2.0 through the [Cybersecurity and Privacy Reference Tool](/projects/cprt/catalog#/cprt/home) in the coming weeks.

**July 22, 2025:**[Proposed updates to the NIST SP 800-53 controls](/Projects/risk-management/sp800-53-controls/public-comments#/search/candidates) addressing secure and reliable patches available for comment through**August 5, 2025** on the NIST SP 800-53 Public Comment Site. See more detail about the changes, view the changes and submit your feedback on the [NIST SP 800-53 Public Comment Site](https://csrc.nist.gov/Projects/risk-management/sp800-53-controls/public-comments#/home).

**June 4, 2025**: NIST invites comments on the initial public draft of SP 800-18r2, [Developing Security, Privacy, and Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management Plans for Systems](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/18/r2/ipd). The public is invited to provide input by **July 30, 2025**.

The NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) provides a comprehensive, flexible, repeatable, and measurable 7-step process that any organization can use to manage information security and privacy risk for organizations and systems and links to a suite of NIST standards and guidelines to support implementation of risk management programs to meet the requirements of the Federal Information Security Modernization Act (FISMA).

---

This site provides an [overview](/Projects/risk-management/about-rmf), explains each RMF step, and offers resources to support implementation, such as updated Quick Start Guides, and the [RMF Publication](https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800-37/rev-2/final).
